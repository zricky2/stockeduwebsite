Learn the terms and slang used by traders everyday

Long

The trader is buying positions in a stock and they expect the stock to go up.

Short

The trader is shorting positions in a stock and they expect the stock to go down.

Cover

The trader buys back his short positions from their broker.

Tape

The combination of "Time & Sales" and Level 2.

Bid

The highest price a buyer is willing to pay for a certain stock.

Ask

The lowest price a seller is willing to take for a certain stock.

DD

An abreviation for Due Diligence. It means for one to do research and take precautions before entering a trade.

Short Squeeze

A circumstance when a stock that has a large number of short positions starts going up and those short sellers start covering their positions. As more short positions are covered, it causes even more positions to be covered in which results in a huge increase in price.

SSR

An abreviation for Short Sell Restriction. It is a circumstance when a stock cannot be shorted unless the stock is going up and only occurs when a stock goes down more than 10% within one day
